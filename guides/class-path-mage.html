Mage Path

Start as Mage if you like spell damage, elemental coverage, mana efficiency, and eventually high-end casters like Omnimancer and Heretic.

Last updated July 1, 2026Caster route

Quick route

Mage → Archmage / Witch or elemental T2 mages → Court Mage → Augur / Mystic / Spellsword → Druid → Spellweaver / Shadowmancer → Archdruid → Nekromancer → Omnimancer → Heretic.

Beginning to end

T1

Mage

Your first spell route. Learn elemental coverage, mana management, and when a resistance swap is better than more magic.

T2

Archmage / Witch, Frost Mage, Scorcher / Scorchess, Warlock

These early classes broaden your spell list. Buy for skills and coverage, then move toward stronger casting passives.

T3-T4

Court Mage, Augur, Mystic, Spellsword

Court Mage keeps you on the caster track. Augur and Mystic add useful magic tools. Spellsword is a hybrid side route if you want weapon-magic play.

T5-T7

Druid, Spellweaver, Shadowmancer, Archdruid

Druid is a major utility pickup. Spellweaver and Archdruid continue the casting route, while Shadowmancer bridges toward Rogue-flavored magic.

T8-T10

Nekromancer / Nekromagus, Omnimancer / Omnimagus, Heretic / Hera

This is the classic high-end caster lane. Plan around magic stats, survivability, status protection, and gear that supports long fights.

Celestial classes

Mage celestial progression branches from Heretic / Hera and is unlocked through Tower Shard spending. Verify current costs and balance in-game before committing.

Heretic Ara / Hera Ara

The celestial magic route. Pick Ara when you want the cleaner caster upgrade path and your gear already supports spell damage and long-fight setup.

Heretic Corvus / Hera Corvus

The evasive Manaflask route. Corvus is more specialized, adds thief equipment access, and fits players who want a trickier caster with more movement away from pure robe-and-staff play.

Shard spending note

Celestial classes compete with celestial weapons, augments, and other tower goals. Read the Towers of Olympia guide before spending Tower Shards.

Side classes worth knowing

Druid is valuable even outside pure Mage. Hybrid classes such as Runeblade, Mystic, and Spellsword can be useful when your gear pushes you away from pure robes and staves.
