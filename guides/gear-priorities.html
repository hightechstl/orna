Gear Priorities

A plainspoken way to decide what to wear, what to upgrade, and when survival is worth more than bigger numbers.

Last updated July 1, 2026Gear guide

Quick rule

The best gear is the gear that wins the content you are actually doing. Damage gear is great until you die. Survival gear is great until fights take forever.

Damage gear vs survival gear

If a fight is safe, add damage. If a fight is inconsistent, add survival first. Consistent clears beat impressive losses.

Ward basics

Ward is a buffer that helps you absorb dangerous turns. Practice it before difficult fights punish you.

Status immunity

Status protection is easy to underrate until it saves the run. Stun, sleep, blight, curse, and similar effects can waste the turn you needed to recover.

Farming gear

Farming gear belongs in its own loadout. Use it when enemies are easy and rewards matter more than safety.

When to upgrade

Upgrade gear that supports your class plan, gives Ward or immunity, or fixes a real problem. Wait on low-quality, off-build, or quickly replaced pieces.

Event gear note

Event gear can be excellent, but availability changes. Check the Codex and current community discussion before ignoring build-defining drops.

Why endgame builds often care about survivability more than raw attack or magic

Endgame content tests whether your build can survive bad turns, status effects, and long fights. A little less attack or magic can be correct if it gives enough Ward, HP, immunity, or defense to keep playing.
