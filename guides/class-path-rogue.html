Rogue Path

Start as Thief if you like speed, extra turns, crit pressure, light gear, and endgame burst routes like Nyx and Realmshifter.

Last updated July 1, 2026Speed route

Quick route

Thief -> Strider / Wanderer -> Shadowmaker -> Mystic or Majestic side tools -> Shadowmancer -> Arcanic -> Nyx -> Realmshifter.

Beginning to end

T1

Thief

The fast starter. You trade safety for speed and damage, so learn when to push and when to wear safer gear.

T2

Strider, Wanderer / Wanderess

These early Rogue-flavored classes teach mobility, light gear habits, and quick-fight pacing.

T3-T5

Shadowmaker, Mystic, Majestic

Shadowmaker keeps the evasive damage identity. Mystic and Majestic are practical side options when you need broader tools.

T6-T8

Shadowmancer, Arcanic

Shadowmancer adds darker magic-adjacent pressure. Arcanic is the major Rogue identity jump and starts feeling like the high-speed route.

T9-T10

Nyx, Realmshifter

Nyx and Realmshifter reward aggressive play, crit planning, and knowing enemy danger. You still need enough survival to actually take your turns.

Celestial classes

Rogue celestial progression branches from Realmshifter and is unlocked through Tower Shard spending. Verify current costs in-game before committing.

Realmshifter Corvus

The critical-hit route. Pick Corvus when your gear and playstyle are built around landing crits, keeping momentum, and turning risky turns into damage.

Realmshifter Dorado

The defensive/evasive route. Dorado gives up some raw offense for more staying power and can use warrior equipment, making it friendlier when hard content is deleting pure glass builds.

Shard spending note

Rogue celestial classes are still endgame investments. Read the Towers of Olympia guide before trading Tower Shards for a sidegrade you may not be geared to use.

Side classes worth knowing

Rogue players often buy defensive or utility side classes because speed alone does not solve towers, raids, or hard dungeons. Do not ignore Ward and status immunity.
