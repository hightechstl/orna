Rogue Path

Start as Thief if you like speed, extra turns, crit pressure, light gear, and endgame burst routes like Nyx and Realmshifter.

Last updated July 1, 2026Speed route

Quick route

Thief -> Strider / Wanderer -> Shadowmaker -> Mystic or Majestic side tools -> Shadowmancer -> Arcanic -> Nyx -> Realmshifter.

Beginning to end

T1

Thief

The fast starter. You trade safety for speed and damage, so learn when to push and when to wear safer gear.

T2

Strider, Wanderer / Wanderess

These early Rogue-flavored classes teach mobility, light gear habits, and quick-fight pacing.

T3-T5

Shadowmaker, Mystic, Majestic

Shadowmaker keeps the evasive damage identity. Mystic and Majestic are practical side options when you need broader tools.

T6-T8

Shadowmancer, Arcanic

Shadowmancer adds darker magic-adjacent pressure. Arcanic is the major Rogue identity jump and starts feeling like the high-speed route.

T9-T10

Nyx, Realmshifter

Nyx and Realmshifter reward aggressive play, crit planning, and knowing enemy danger. You still need enough survival to actually take your turns.

Celestial classes

Rogue celestial progression branches from Realmshifter and is unlocked through Tower Shard spending. Verify current costs in-game before committing.

Realmshifter Corvus

The critical-hit route. Pick Corvus when your gear and playstyle are built around landing crits, keeping momentum, and turning risky turns into damage.

Realmshifter Dorado

The defensive/evasive route. Dorado gives up some raw offense for more staying power and can use warrior equipment, making it friendlier when hard content is deleting pure glass builds.

Shard spending note

Rogue celestial classes are still endgame investments. Read the Towers of Olympia guide before trading Tower Shards for a sidegrade you may not be geared to use.

Community build variants

These are practical endgame templates distilled from player discussions on r/OrnaRPG, not fixed loadouts. Treat them as priorities to test against your gear, ascension level, amities, and current patch notes.

Realmshifter Corvus: crit-chain pressure

Best fit: raids, endless-style farming, PvP pressure, and dungeons where your crit chain can stay online. Corvus is the aggressive Rogue pick once you can support high crit chance without giving up every layer of survival.

Build around: crit chance, crit damage, attack, dexterity, and enough ward or HP to survive setup turns. Community examples point toward crit-adorned thief gear, Riftrogue-style boots when available, and Celestial Bow or Greatbow routes using Prometheus Hands for more crit value.

Play pattern: get your crit engine moving, then chain kills or raid turns before the enemy can punish the low-safety setup. Official balance notes also make Corvus more dungeon-friendly by letting its crit chain carry between floors.

Watch out: the expensive version asks for multiple unlocks and a tuned celestial weapon. If you are dying before the chain matters, use base Realmshifter or Dorado until the gear catches up.

Realmshifter Dorado: dex tower duelist

Best fit: towers, regular PvP, horde dungeons, and players who want Realmshifter damage without playing pure glass. Reddit Dorado advice repeatedly centers on making the class evasive and consistent before chasing maximum burst.

Build around: dexterity first, then ward, HP, crit chance, and attack. Common community priorities include a Celestial Axe plus Chained Shield for towers and dungeons, a two-handed Celestial Bow for PvP, status-protection accessories, and enough dex to turn Dorado's passive into real avoidance.

Play pattern: lean on Avidity III, dex, and ward tools to stay ahead in turn economy. For tower climbing, safer shield setups are often preferred; for PvP, high crit and dex can turn Dorado into a fast duelist.

Watch out: Dorado is versatile, but not automatically best at raids or endless. Keep Corvus or base Realmshifter available when the content rewards raw crit farming more than defensive consistency.

Side classes worth knowing

Rogue players often buy defensive or utility side classes because speed alone does not solve towers, raids, or hard dungeons. Do not ignore Ward and status immunity.
