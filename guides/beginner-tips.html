Beginner Tips

Early choices that keep progression smooth, reduce wasted upgrades, and make the game less confusing.

Last updated July 1, 2026Starter guide

Quick start

Save orns for classes, build useful shops near your origin town, and learn Ward before the game forces you to.

Join a kingdom

A kingdom gives you raids, wars, advice, and a reason to check in. Even a casual kingdom is better than playing in a vacuum.

Build shops and blacksmith near origin town

Keep routine maintenance close. Shops help with supplies, and a blacksmith gives you a convenient upgrade loop.

Save orns for class progression

Classes are usually the best long-term use of orns. Falling behind on class progression hurts more than skipping a luxury purchase.

Do daily quests

Daily quests add steady rewards and structure to short sessions.

Use dungeons

Dungeons are a clean way to turn play time into progress. Bring supplies and start with safer modes until your gear can handle pressure.

Upgrade only gear worth keeping

Upgrade pieces that solve a real problem or will stay useful for a while. Save materials when a drop is only a tiny bump.

Learn Ward early

Ward teaches you how to survive hard turns, and later builds often assume you understand it.

Keep multiple loadouts

Keep a damage setup, a safer setup, and a farming setup when you can. Switching intentionally is better than forcing one build everywhere.

Do not waste rare resources before understanding gear tiers

Slow down before spending rare materials, Tower Shards, event currency, or large orn piles. Learn what gets replaced quickly and what supports your long-term plan.

Verify before committing

If a resource is hard to replace, check the Codex, ask current players, or test the build first.
